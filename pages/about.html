About!

The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development (OECD) defines income inequality as “a measure that highlights the gap between different individuals’ or households’ disposable income.” Within the United States, this gap continues to grow significantly. According to figures published by the Congressional Budget Office (CBO), the average income of the richest households increased nine times as fast as the income of the poorest households. Additional statistics show that the richest households' income is more than 84 times greater than the poorest households. Both income inequality and income concentration have returned to the levels present in the United States during the Gilded Age.
As one of the wealthiest nations in the world, there is no reason the United States should maintain such a large income inequality. With proper social spending and taxes on wealthy corporations, we can live in a more equal future. Till then…
